Graphic Design

Balance Brochure Design

Project Overview

This project is to create a brochure for a fictional play “Balance” based on the information provided in the Graphic Design course at BCIT. (MDIA 1905). The main goal of this project is to design a brochure that goes well with the theme of the play and provide details about the play to the audience using design principles.

Timeline Mar 19, 2020 – Apr 02, 2020
Role Graphic Designer
Tools Adobe InDesign, Adobe Photoshop
Backstory This is a story from Japanese writer Yoko Matsuda, a portrait of a segment of the population that tends to get the least attention in the media and people: the working poor. In telling the story of a loving, piecemeal family who resorts to shoplifting noodles and fishing poles so they can live off their inconsistent incomes, Balance demands empathy for people who many, especially in the news media, prefer to demonize.
01

Brainstorm

First, I brainstormed the ideas using the mind mapping software Xmind to decide how I could deliver the message of the play which is social injustice using the main symbols of the story and match the design with the theme.

02

Sketch

To generate a broad range of ideas, I used conecpt thumbnails and rough thumbnails. For the concept thumbnails, I narrowed down my ten ideas to six and sketched the main elements and concept. For the rough thumbnails, I chose concept 6 and created six variations of it.

03

Final Product

To maximize the contrast between the rich and the working poor, I chose to go with black and white colour only, and used noodles and one dollar bill which create clear contrast to represent economic inequality between them.
